Privacy First

Protecting personal information isn’t just a feature — it’s our foundation. Neurodiverse individuals and families deserve tools that respect dignity, safety, and autonomy. This page outlines our principles, controls, and technical approach.

Why it matters

  • Sensitive contexts: Families and practitioners may share health, education, and behavioral data that must be safeguarded.
  • Trust drives adoption: Clear consent, control, and transparency are essential to sustained use.
  • Ethics + compliance: We align our design with relevant U.S./EU privacy frameworks (e.g., HIPAA/FERPA scope, GDPR concepts).

Our principles

  • Minimal data collection: Only what’s essential for the service. No dark patterns.
  • Explicit consent: Clear language and granular toggles for sensitive features.
  • User control: Access, export, and deletion flows available from Settings.
  • Security by default: Encryption in transit/at rest; key management and access logs.

Data lifecycle (overview)

  • Collect: Limited, purpose-bound inputs with just-in-time notices.
  • Store: Encrypted databases with role-based access; least-privilege policies.
  • Process: Privacy-preserving defaults; audit trails for sensitive operations.
  • Share: Only with explicit consent or necessary processors under DPAs.
  • Delete: User-initiated deletion and time-bounded retention for backups.

Controls you can expect

  • Privacy dashboard with Download my data, Delete my account, and consent toggles.
  • Clear, human‑readable privacy notices at data collection points.
  • In‑product signals for when data leaves the device or is shared with a processor.
